About



The mission of the European Set Theory Society is to strengthen set-theoretical research and scholarship in Europe. Although its activities are centred in Europe, the Society is open to set theorists worldwide.


Set theory is the study of the mathematical infinite. It was born in the late 19th century with Georg Cantor’s mathematical theory of transfinite numbers, and after its axiomatization by Zermelo and others in the early 20th century it developed into a general theory of infinite sets with numerous connections and applications in areas such as algebra, topology, real and complex analysis, functional analysis, measure theory, and theoretical computer science.


In the last years, set theory has witnessed a renaissance with the creation of several new research groups and a substantial increase in the number of researchers.
